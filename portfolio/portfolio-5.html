          \item $\{\frac{1}{n} | n \in \mathbb{N}\} \cup \{0\}$
          \item $\mathbb{R}$
          \item $\mathbb{Q}$
          \item $\mathbb{Z}$
          \item $\emptyset$
      \end{enumerate}

  \item[Problem 4.3] Prove that every open interval is an open set.

  \item[Problem 4.4] Provide an example of an open set that is not a single open interval.

  \item[Problem 4.5] Prove that if $U$ and $V$ are open sets, then
      \begin{enumerate}
          \item $U \cup V$ is an open set, and
          \item $U \cap V$ is an open set.
      \end{enumerate}
  \item[Solution 4.5]

  \item[Problem 4.6] Prove that if $\{U_\alpha\}_{\alpha \in \Delta}$ is a collection of open sets, then $\bigcup_{\alpha \in \Delta} U_\alpha$ is an open set.
  \item[Solution 4.6]

  \item[Problem 4.7] Prove that if $\{U_i\}_{i=1}^{n}$ is a finite collection of open sets for $n \in \mathbb{N}$, then $\bigcap_{i=1}^n U_i$ is an open set.
  \item[Solution 4.7]

  \item[Problem 4.8] Explain why we cannot utilize induction to prove that the intersection of infinitely many open sets indexed by the natural numbers is open.
